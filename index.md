intro_text: |
  I am a biophotonics engineer and Duke University Ph.D. graduate specializing in optical imaging and medical device design. My doctoral research focused on detecting Alzheimer’s Disease biomarkers using low-cost OCT guided angle-resolved Low Coherence Interferometry (a/LCI). During this time, I designed a Gen 3 low-cost balanced-detection OCT system for _in vivo_ retinal imaging and conducted the first human _in vivo_ retinal a/LCI clinical study. My technical foundation spans over a decade across [Duke's BIOS lab](https://bios.bme.duke.edu/) and UNC-Chapel Hill’s [Optical Coherence Imaging Lab](https://cil.sites.oasis.unc.edu/), where I developed expertise in optomechanics, 3D anatomical reconstruction from OCT and CT scans, and advanced modalities like high-throughput holographic cytometry. Beyond the optics bench, you can find me making my famous chocolate chip cookies, hanging out with my friends, or cheering on the UNC Tar Heels!
