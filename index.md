intro_text : 
  After discovering an interest in biophotonics and medical devices in high school, I began working in Adam Wax's (BIOS) lab in 2009-2012 at Duke University. Here I learned basic opto-mechanincs, microscopy, fiber optics, MATLAB, imageJ, clean room work. I studied Physics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill from 2010-2015 earning a Bachelors of Science degree. While at UNC I began working in Amy Josefsberg's (formerly Oldenburg) Optical Coherence Imaging Lab. There I focused on image processing and 3D reconstruction of airway geometry from anatomical Optical Coherence Tomography (aOCT) images. After graduating from UNC, I continued on as a Research Specialist in the Coherence Imaging Lab focusing on validating 3D reconstruction of phantoms and in vivo pig airways built from aOCT and CT scans. In 2019 I returned to the BIOS lab as a Research Assistant to work on Dual-Axis OCT, combined Quantitative Phase Imaging (QPI) and Forrester Resonance Energy Transfer imaging, High throughput holographic cytometry, and OCT guided a/LCI optical biopsy. In 2021 I began his PhD studies at Duke with a research focus on using low-cost OCT and angle-resolved Low Coherence Interferometry (a/LCI) to detect biomarkers of Alzheimer's Disease in a clinical setting. In his free time I enjoy socializing, cooking, and cheering on UNC Tar Heels.
